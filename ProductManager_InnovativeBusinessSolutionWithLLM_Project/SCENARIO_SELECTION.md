# Scenario Selection

## Chosen Scenario
Scenario A: In-Car AI Assistant Service

## Role and Company Context
- Role: Product Manager on the digital services team at a major automaker.
- Product baseline: legacy voice-activated navigation/information service with outdated UX.
- Business objective: attract younger, tech-savvy urban/suburban buyers while avoiding privacy and safety backlash.

## Why Scenario A Was Selected
- Strong strategic upside: modern in-car assistant experience can improve vehicle differentiation and subscription attach rates.
- Rich data context: new vehicles already collect relevant contextual signals.
- High-stakes constraints: privacy, driver distraction, and resilience must be designed in from day one.

## Target User Profile (Given Data)
- Age: 25-45
- Income: $60k-$130k
- Location: top 20 US metro areas
- Profession: knowledge workers
- Device split: 75% iOS, 25% Android
- Digital behavior: 98% messaging, 80% user-generated video, 65% music streaming, 40% video streaming
- Mobility pattern: 15 private-car trips/week, average 12 miles, 75% work trips
- AI familiarity: 50% already use conversational AI

## Problem Statement
The current assistant cannot meet modern expectations for natural, personalized, and context-aware interactions. If not modernized, the company risks reduced competitiveness in a segment where conversational AI expectations are rapidly increasing.

## Opportunity Statement
Build a privacy-first, safety-gated, hybrid edge+cloud LLM assistant that delivers natural in-car interactions while minimizing distraction, preserving trust, and remaining resilient during poor network conditions.

## Reference Signals
- Privacy scrutiny in connected cars has increased significantly in media and policy discussion.
- Poor data governance can trigger consumer backlash, regulatory pressure, and reputational damage.
